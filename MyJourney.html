My Web Dev Journey so far...
(Coding is boring??!)

01 Dec, 2020

For me Web Development was not at all part of my life. I didn't even know its an option that existed. I just knew that CODING IS BORING! So how did it become a thing that i love to do now you may ask? So during the time of lockdown i did some research and came across some people who were saying in todays world how skills are gonna be overtaking degrees. I mean thats a discusion for another time but i did agreed with them on some level. After my research i learned photoshop for 2 months and then thought of switching to web development because 'they say' one can earn a fortune in the field of programming, yes thats right, money was the primary motivation for me to learn programming.

I Started by leaning HTML,CSS made a couple of web pages. After a having a good grip on HTML and CSS i started learning Javascript which i felt fairly tough at first. Then onwards i started creating simple application/games. It took a hell lot of a time for me to make each of them, it was getting frustrating at times but when the final product gets ready it feels total worth it. Thats when i realized i love creating and designing stuff, making things work is what i love to do. To improve my logical thinking i started doing problem solving exercises. Ofcouse none of them were easy, I've beaten my head over many times if i cant get it right, I've cried if anything doesnt seem to work, I've dropped many projects if i didnt know what should i use to work it out or i was too lazy complete it. I still do all of these things but one thing i've realized is i love failing and keep trying in this arena. I still have long long way to go and one day i want to be recognised as well established web developer.

Here are some of my observations/learnings I've picked up on my journey so far.. Just a disclaimer this may not apply to you because am still a beginner in this field.

  1. You will have to give yourself some hard time.Its definitly not easy but its fun.
  2. If you dont know something, Google got your answers.
  3. Try to learn things you dont know in-depth. Dont just learn the top layer, dig a little deeper.
  4. Be Clam and Have patience. In stress you sometimes overlook the picture and miss the silly mistake you might have made.
  5. You wont learn by watching, you learn by actually coding.
  6. Whenever you are not coding do something fun and relaxing.
  7. Join communities with people better tham you, ask help and doubts if feel stuck.
  8. 1 hour daily practice is better than 5 hours in every 3 three days
  9. Dont be insecure if someone is better, be inspired.

My perception that coding is boring has completely changed. In my opinion its only boring if you make it boring. If you set a goal of making an app/game, you'll love the process that's what's important.
